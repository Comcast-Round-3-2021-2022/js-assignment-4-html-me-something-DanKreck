VanScan Application

A brief overview of an iPhone application I made that solved a problem.

The Situation

Years back, when technicians were tasked with logging their inventory, the procedure was very new. Supervisors were scanning our inventory one vehicle at a time. This obviously wasnt productive, and was causing a potential for missed appointments and a poor customer experience.

The Task

Having some background with Android applications, I decided I was going to attempt to make an iPhone application that solved this issue. After a few weeks of research and a lot of trial and error, I was able to compile a working application that offered a solution.

The Action

I named the application "VanScan". I installed the app onto my phone, and began testing the workflow with other employees in the reconciliation department. I asked them what issues they were facing and what this application might be able to do to assist them. After a few suggestions, I added two features:

  1. Providing the NT Login username in the file name.
  2. Exporting the inventory results as an Excel file.

The Result

VanScan proved to be very effective and saved me a lot of time in my role as a technician. The app caught the attention of a national director, and we discussed the possibility of having VanScan uploaded to the internal app catalog. I continued to use VanScan for a few months, until Comcast offered a 3rd party solution. The new solution offered more options such as the ability to receive, lookup status, etc. Because of this, my app was essentially obsolete. However, I have already found areas of improvement in the current process, and I believe we could offer a better solution within Tech360, that would render the current process obsolete.
